Willing the Way
Nov 01 '14

This week's cultural blog is determining which value you place the most importance on. For me, that is will-power. I find that the times I'm the happiest or most proud of is when I can control what I want to do when I want to do it. Will-power unwavering! I feel like having a strong will is conducive to getting things done; especially when you don't particularly enjoy a task, or when you're feeling lazy and unwilling to just do what needs to be done. Being able to push through the struggle and attaining your goals. I find that there are so many distractions in life, and staying focused can be trying. That's when will-power comes in and kicks you into motion or keeps you on track. I've realised that I tend to procrastinate often, so exercising my will-power is like making a promise to myself and when I fulfill that promise, it brings me joy. I like surprising myself that way. I don't like being lazy; it may give me some satisfaction, but it is short lived. Life doesn't come with a pause button, eventually tasks pile up and start to hit the fan. I've learned that the salient key to success is delayed gratification.

In general, I try to be conscious of my will power. It's just a matter of practice and exercise. There are times when I struggle and become lax, so it's important to me to reduce the amount of times this happens. Time is short. You need to make the most of it.

Having a strong will stops the harpies from luring you into temptation. So does having friends tie you to a pole. Some time ago, a friend asked me about relationships. How do you stay committed? I believe will plays a big role here. You need to know what it is that you want. Are you willing to work through something, or dump it all and give it up?

Speaking of giving it all up. I've been scrambling to catch up with my DBC readings. There are so many books to read that when I see the daunting list, I feel overwhelmed. Luckily that's when I think about will-power. Pow! Get it done. One page at a time. This is what you have chosen to do. Enjoy the journey, even if you're stumbling through it.

Last week my cultural blog was about stereotype threats. Having a strong will definitely helps overcome these odds. A strong belief in your own identity sifts the general criticism into objective criticism and illuminates areas where you can improve.
